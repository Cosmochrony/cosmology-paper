\subsection{Physical Interpretation of the Saturation Amplitude}
  \label{subsec:physical-interpretation-of-the-saturation-amplitude}

  The parameter $f_{\max}$ does not represent an adjustable fit coefficient,
  but the maximal admissible fractional deficit compatible with the bounded-response
  structure defined by Eq.~(1).

  Consistency with galactic dynamics constrains its range.
  Values $f_{\max} \ll 0.05$ fail to reproduce the observed MOND-like
  transition in low-surface-brightness galaxies,
  while values $f_{\max} \gtrsim 0.15$ would induce measurable
  departures from Newtonian behavior in high-acceleration environments.

  The admissible interval $0.05 \lesssim f_{\max} \lesssim 0.15$
  is therefore fixed by internal cross-scale consistency,
  rather than by cosmological tuning.

  Within this range, the void-induced fractional shift
  $\Delta H/H \simeq \delta_\rho f_{\max}$
  naturally falls at the percent level for observed
  void contrasts $\delta_\rho \sim 0.2$--$0.4$,
  without requiring additional degrees of freedom.
