\subsection{Summary}
  \label{subsec:robustness_summary}

  The effective saturation framework remains stable under reasonable
  variations of baryonic mass-to-light ratios and does not rely on
  galaxy-dependent halo parameters.
  The acceleration scale $a_\star$ is determined directly from
  rotation curves and subsequently applied, without readjustment,
  to late-time low-density environments.

  At cosmological scales, the mechanism does not modify the background
  Friedmann expansion.
  The predicted offset in locally inferred $H_0$ arises from an
  environment-dependent saturation of the effective kinematic inference,
  rather than from additional energy components or altered gravitational
  dynamics.

  The proposal is therefore both constrained and falsifiable.
  Its viability depends on the simultaneous validity of:
  (i) a universal saturation scale across disk galaxies,
  and
  (ii) a measurable correlation between locally inferred expansion rates
  and large-scale density contrast.
  Failure of either condition would invalidate the framework.
