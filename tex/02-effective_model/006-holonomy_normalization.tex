\subsection{Holonomy Normalization of $\kappa$}
  \label{subsec:holonomy_normalization}

  The normalization factor $\kappa$ can be fixed by consistency with the
  $\mathrm{U}(1)$ fiber structure established in the companion Born\textendash Infeld analysis.

  In the saturated regime, the effective connection amplitude is uniquely determined by scale invariance as
  \begin{equation}
    |A_T| \sim \frac{a_\star}{c^2}.
  \end{equation}

  Consider the canonical cosmological loop of circumference $L_H = 2\pi R_H = 2\pi c/H_0$.
  The accumulated holonomy is
  \begin{equation}
    \Delta\theta_H
    \sim \frac{a_\star}{c^2} \cdot 2\pi R_H
    = \frac{2\pi a_\star}{cH_0}.
  \end{equation}

  Two intrinsic saturation criteria arise:

  \begin{itemize}
    \item Topological closure: $\Delta\theta_H = 2\pi$,
    giving $a_\star = cH_0$.
    \item Coherence degradation: $\Delta\theta_H = 1$,
    giving $a_\star = cH_0/(2\pi)$.
  \end{itemize}

  The ratio between the two thresholds is fixed by the $\mathrm{U}(1)$ topology and is not adjusted.
