Dear Editors,

Please find attached our manuscript entitled
“A Single Effective Mechanism for Galaxy Rotation Curves and the Hubble Tension”
submitted for consideration as an Article in the Special Issue
“Tensions, Anomalies, and Challenges in the Standard Cosmological Model”.

This work investigates whether two long-standing observational anomalies—flat galaxy rotation curves and the Hubble
constant tension—may originate from a common effective mechanism operating in low-density environments.
The manuscript introduces a minimal phenomenological framework in which departures from Newtonian expectations arise
from a bounded effective gravitational response at the level of kinematic inference, without modifying early-universe
cosmology or introducing additional dark components.

In the galactic regime, the framework reproduces the main features of observed rotation curves across a wide range of
galaxy types using baryonic matter alone, with a single saturation scale fixed from the SPARC sample.
At cosmological scales, the same bounded-response structure leads to an environment-dependent bias in the locally
inferred expansion rate in deep voids. An explicit order-of-magnitude estimate shows that the deep-saturation regime
applies for observationally inferred void parameters, yielding a fractional shift of the correct magnitude without
introducing new cosmological degrees of freedom.

The central aim of the manuscript is not to replace the standard cosmological model, but to examine whether a
structurally consistent late-time effective mechanism may account simultaneously for distinct low-density anomalies
while preserving the background Friedmann evolution and early-universe constraints. The paper also discusses robustness,
 observational degeneracies, and specific empirical tests, including environment-dependent signatures in void regions
 and high-redshift compact sources.

We believe the manuscript aligns closely with the objectives of the Special Issue by addressing cosmological tensions
within a controlled and quantitatively testable framework.

This work is original, has not been published elsewhere, and is not under consideration by another journal.
All authors have approved the manuscript.
